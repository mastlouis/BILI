BILI 2020 Retreat

Visible and Invisible:

Examining Identities

About our Spring Retreat

To what extent do you feel like your religious/philosophical identities/worldview are visible in your day-to-day life? How often do you think about it, and in what circumstances? How do your identities, or your relationship to your identities, impact the way that you approach others and understand yourself? We will explore these questions and more at our annual retreat!

Where & When: The retreat is the weekend of February 22nd and February 23rd, 2020 at the MIT Endicott House in Deadham, Massachusetts. How to get there: We will coordinate carpooling. Attendees are encouraged to use ride shares. BILI is supported by a grant from the Arthur Vining Davis Foundations

About BILI

We are team of local undergraduate students from Bentley University, Boston University, Boston College, Brown University, MIT, Northeastern University, Tufts University and Wellesley College with a commitment to interreligious and cross-cultural engagement on our college campuses.

This gathering is sponsored by the Miller Center for Interreligious Learning and Leadership of Hebrew College and supported by a grant from the Of Many Institute for Multifaith Leadership at NYU.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this Event really free?

Yes, this retreat includes free accommodations, meals, and linens.

The only thing not covered by BILI will be transportation to and from the retreat.

What are the housing arrangements?

Everyone will be assigned and individual bedroom and may share a common room and bathroom with no more than 3 others.

What is the MIT Endicott Mansion?

Surrounded by 25 acres of magnificent gardens and grounds, the Endicott House is a mansion built in 1934 in the style of a French chateau.
